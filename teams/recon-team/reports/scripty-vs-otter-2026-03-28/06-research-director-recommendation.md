# Report 6: Research Director's Final Recommendation
**Agent:** Research Director
**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Mission:** Scripty vs. Otter.ai — Should Scripty compete?

---

## Verdict: **CONDITIONAL-GO** — With Specific Positioning

---

## The Decision

**Do NOT position Scripty as an Otter.ai competitor.** You will lose that fight. Otter owns "meeting transcription" with $100M ARR, WSJ recognition, and enterprise customers.

**DO position Scripty as the "Capture-to-Learning" platform for multilingual students.** This is a niche that:
- Otter doesn't serve (they serve sales teams)
- NotebookLM partially serves (but requires manual text upload, no transcription)
- No single tool fully serves today

---

## Who Made the Stronger Case

**The Realist Angel wins**, but the Devil's warnings are guardrails:

| Decision | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| **Target audience:** Multilingual students, not business professionals | Otter owns business. Students are underserved. Scripty's pricing/features align. |
| **Positioning:** "Record in any language. Study in yours." | Translation is the killer feature no competitor has. Lead with it. |
| **First feature to ship:** Study guide templates (Phase 1) | 1-2 weeks using existing infrastructure. Immediate differentiation. |
| **Viral feature:** Audio study podcasts (Phase 2) | Proven by NotebookLM. Scripty's advantage: no manual upload needed. |
| **Do NOT build:** CRM integration, sales coaching, live meeting bots | That's Otter's territory. Stay in your lane. |
